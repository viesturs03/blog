What Is SRE, Really?

SRE ‚Äî or Site Reliability Engineering ‚Äî is a discipline that originated at Google, combining software engineering principles with infrastructure and operations to build scalable and highly reliable systems. It was created as a response to a growing need: how can large-scale services be operated reliably without slowing down innovation?

Instead of traditional system administrators managing uptime reactively, SREs use code to automate operations and enforce service-level standards. They don‚Äôt just maintain systems ‚Äî they design them to be resilient and self-healing, often pushing back on unrealistic availability goals to maintain a healthy balance between speed and stability.

SLOs, SLAs, and Error Budgets

A major focus of the lecture was the framework of Service-Level Objectives (SLOs), Service-Level Agreements (SLAs), and Error Budgets. These concepts help bridge the gap between business expectations and technical reliability.

  • SLA (Service-Level Agreement): This is the formal contract with the client. It defines what level of availability or performance they are promised.
  • SLO (Service-Level Objective): These are internal targets, typically stricter than SLAs, used to guide engineering decisions.
  • Error Budget: This is the allowable margin of failure within the SLO. It‚Äôs essentially a buffer that acknowledges perfection isn‚Äôt sustainable ‚Äî and that some failure is okay if it allows for faster iteration and innovation.

This approach flips the traditional mindset: instead of striving for 100% uptime, SRE encourages teams to ask, how reliable does this service need to be? It‚Äôs a practical philosophy that balances risk and reward.

SRE vs DevOps: A Complement, Not a Competitor

Another key takeaway was the distinction between SRE and DevOps. While both aim to improve collaboration between development and operations, SRE formalizes this with engineering-backed principles and metrics. Where DevOps promotes culture and shared responsibility, SRE operationalizes it with automation, observability, and objective thresholds for success.

In a way, DevOps is the mindset ‚Äî SRE is the method.
